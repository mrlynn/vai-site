# CCPA Compliance Summary: Acme Corp

**Document ID:** ACME-LEGAL-CCPA-2024-002
**Effective Date:** January 15, 2024
**Last Reviewed:** November 1, 2024
**Classification:** Internal, Confidential

## 1. Applicability

1.1 The California Consumer Privacy Act of 2018, as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act of 2020 (collectively, "CCPA"), applies to Acme Corp ("the Company") as a for-profit entity that does business in California and meets one or more of the following thresholds: (a) annual gross revenues exceeding twenty-five million dollars ($25,000,000); (b) annually buying, selling, or sharing the personal information of one hundred thousand (100,000) or more California residents, households, or devices; or (c) deriving fifty percent (50%) or more of annual revenue from selling or sharing California consumers' personal information.

1.2 Acme Corp processes personal information of California residents through its cloud-based platform and associated services. This document outlines the Company's obligations under the CCPA and the mechanisms through which it ensures compliance.

1.3 For purposes of this document, "consumer" means a natural person who is a California resident, as defined in Section 17014 of Title 18 of the California Code of Regulations.

## 2. Consumer Rights

2.1 **Right to Know (Section 1798.100):** Consumers have the right to request that the Company disclose the categories and specific pieces of personal information it has collected about them, the categories of sources from which personal information was collected, the business or commercial purpose for collecting or selling personal information, and the categories of third parties with which personal information is shared.

2.2 **Right to Delete (Section 1798.105):** Consumers have the right to request the deletion of personal information that the Company has collected from them, subject to certain exceptions. Exceptions include situations where the information is necessary to complete a transaction, detect security incidents, exercise free speech, comply with a legal obligation, or conduct research in the public interest.

2.3 **Right to Opt-Out of Sale or Sharing (Section 1798.120):** Consumers have the right to direct the Company not to sell or share their personal information to third parties. Acme Corp provides a "Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information" link on its website and within its platform settings. The Company shall respect browser-based opt-out signals, including the Global Privacy Control ("GPC"), as a valid opt-out request.

2.4 **Right to Correct (Section 1798.106):** Consumers have the right to request that the Company correct inaccurate personal information that it maintains about them. The Company shall use commercially reasonable efforts to correct the information as directed by the consumer.

2.5 **Right to Limit Use of Sensitive Personal Information (Section 1798.121):** Where the Company collects or processes sensitive personal information, consumers may direct the Company to limit its use to that which is necessary to perform the services or provide the goods reasonably expected by an average consumer.

2.6 **Right to Non-Discrimination (Section 1798.125):** The Company shall not discriminate against a consumer for exercising any of their CCPA rights. Discrimination includes denying goods or services, charging different prices, providing a different level of quality, or suggesting that the consumer will receive a different price or quality.

## 3. Categories of Personal Information

3.1 Acme Corp collects and processes the following categories of personal information as defined in Section 1798.140(v):

3.1.1 Identifiers such as name, email address, account name, and IP address.

3.1.2 Commercial information including records of services purchased, obtained, or considered, and purchasing histories.

3.1.3 Internet or other electronic network activity information, including browsing history, search history, and information regarding a consumer's interaction with the Company's platform.

3.1.4 Geolocation data derived from IP addresses or user-provided location information.

3.1.5 Professional or employment-related information provided in connection with enterprise accounts.

3.1.6 Inferences drawn from the above categories to create a profile reflecting preferences, characteristics, and behavior.

## 4. Service Provider Obligations

4.1 Acme Corp engages third-party service providers who process personal information on its behalf. Each service provider is contractually obligated to: (a) process personal information only for the specific business purposes set forth in the written agreement; (b) refrain from selling or sharing the personal information; (c) refrain from combining personal information received from the Company with personal information collected from other sources, except as permitted by the CCPA; and (d) comply with the CCPA and provide the same level of privacy protection as required by the statute.

4.2 The Company conducts annual assessments of its service providers to verify ongoing compliance with these contractual obligations and the requirements of the CCPA.

## 5. Sale of Personal Information

5.1 Acme Corp does not sell personal information in the traditional sense. However, the Company recognizes that certain data-sharing activities with third-party analytics and advertising partners may constitute a "sale" or "sharing" under the CCPA's broad definitions.

5.2 Where such activities are identified, the Company provides consumers with the ability to opt out through the mechanisms described in Section 2.3 of this document.

5.3 The Company does not have actual knowledge that it sells or shares the personal information of consumers under the age of sixteen (16). In the event such activity is identified, the Company shall obtain affirmative authorization in accordance with Section 1798.120(c).

## 6. Verification Requirements

6.1 Upon receiving a consumer request to know or delete personal information, Acme Corp shall verify the identity of the requesting consumer to a reasonable degree of certainty. The verification process is proportional to the sensitivity of the information involved.

6.2 For requests to know categories of personal information, the Company shall match at least two (2) data points provided by the consumer with data points maintained by the Company.

6.3 For requests to know specific pieces of personal information or for deletion requests, the Company shall match at least three (3) data points and require a signed declaration under penalty of perjury that the requestor is the consumer whose personal information is the subject of the request.

6.4 If the Company cannot reasonably verify the identity of the consumer making the request, it shall inform the consumer and explain why it cannot verify their identity, along with guidance on how the consumer may provide sufficient verification.

6.5 Acme Corp shall respond to verified consumer requests within forty-five (45) calendar days of receipt, with the possibility of an additional forty-five (45) day extension where reasonably necessary, provided the consumer is notified of the extension.
