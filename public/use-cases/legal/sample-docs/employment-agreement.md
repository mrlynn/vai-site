# Employment Agreement

**Acme Corp**
**Effective Date:** January 15, 2025

This Employment Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into by and between Acme Corp, a Delaware corporation with its principal place of business at 400 Technology Drive, Suite 200, San Francisco, California 94105 ("Company"), and the individual identified on the signature page hereto ("Employee").

## 1. Position and Duties

1.1 The Company hereby employs Employee in the position set forth in the attached Schedule A, or such other position as the Company may assign from time to time.

1.2 Employee shall devote full professional time, attention, and best efforts to the performance of duties assigned by the Company. Employee shall comply with all Company policies, procedures, and directives as established or modified from time to time at the Company's sole discretion.

1.3 Employee shall report to the supervisor designated by the Company and shall perform such duties as are customarily associated with the assigned position, together with any additional duties reasonably requested by the Company.

1.4 During the term of employment, Employee shall not engage in any other employment, consulting, or business activity that would conflict with Employee's obligations to the Company or that would impair Employee's ability to fulfill those obligations.

## 2. Compensation and Benefits

2.1 **Base Salary.** The Company shall pay Employee an annual base salary as specified in Schedule A, payable in accordance with the Company's standard payroll schedule and subject to applicable withholdings and deductions.

2.2 **Performance Bonus.** Employee may be eligible for an annual performance bonus as determined by the Company in its sole discretion. Any bonus payment is contingent upon Employee's continued employment through the date of payment and satisfactory performance as evaluated by the Company.

2.3 **Equity Compensation.** Subject to approval by the Company's Board of Directors, Employee may be granted equity awards under the Company's equity incentive plan, subject to the terms and conditions of the applicable plan documents and award agreements.

2.4 **Benefits.** Employee shall be eligible to participate in the Company's standard employee benefit programs, including health insurance, dental and vision coverage, retirement savings plans, and paid time off, subject to the terms and conditions of each program as they may be amended from time to time.

2.5 **Expense Reimbursement.** The Company shall reimburse Employee for reasonable and necessary business expenses incurred in the performance of Employee's duties, provided that such expenses are approved in advance and documented in accordance with Company policy.

## 3. Confidentiality

3.1 Employee acknowledges that during the course of employment, Employee will have access to and become acquainted with Confidential Information belonging to the Company. "Confidential Information" includes, without limitation, trade secrets, proprietary data, customer lists, financial information, business strategies, product roadmaps, technical specifications, source code, algorithms, and any other information not generally known to the public.

3.2 Employee agrees to hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence and shall not, during or after the term of employment, disclose, publish, or otherwise reveal any Confidential Information to any third party except as required in the performance of Employee's duties or as authorized in writing by the Company.

3.3 Upon termination of employment for any reason, Employee shall promptly return to the Company all documents, materials, and property containing or relating to Confidential Information, including all copies, summaries, and excerpts thereof.

## 4. Non-Competition

4.1 During the term of employment and for a period of twelve (12) months following the termination of employment for any reason ("Restricted Period"), Employee shall not, directly or indirectly, engage in, own, manage, operate, control, be employed by, consult for, or participate in the ownership, management, operation, or control of any business that competes with the Company's business within the geographic territories in which the Company operates.

4.2 For purposes of this Section, a "competing business" means any enterprise that develops, markets, sells, or provides products or services substantially similar to or competitive with those offered by the Company at the time of Employee's termination.

4.3 Employee acknowledges that this restriction is reasonable in scope, duration, and geographic area, and that the Company has provided adequate consideration for this covenant, including the employment and compensation described herein.

## 5. Non-Solicitation

5.1 During the Restricted Period, Employee shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit, recruit, or encourage any employee, consultant, or independent contractor of the Company to terminate their relationship with the Company.

5.2 During the Restricted Period, Employee shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit, divert, or attempt to divert any customer, client, or business partner of the Company for the purpose of providing products or services that compete with those offered by the Company.

## 6. Termination

6.1 **At-Will Employment.** Employee's employment with the Company is at-will, meaning that either party may terminate the employment relationship at any time, with or without cause, and with or without notice, subject to applicable law.

6.2 **Termination by Company for Cause.** The Company may terminate Employee's employment immediately for Cause. "Cause" includes, without limitation: (a) material breach of this Agreement; (b) conviction of a felony or crime involving moral turpitude; (c) willful misconduct or gross negligence in the performance of duties; (d) fraud, embezzlement, or misappropriation of Company assets; or (e) repeated failure to perform assigned duties after written notice and a reasonable opportunity to cure.

6.3 **Severance.** In the event that the Company terminates Employee's employment without Cause, the Company shall provide Employee with severance pay equal to the number of months specified in Schedule A at Employee's then-current base salary rate, contingent upon Employee's execution of a general release of claims in a form satisfactory to the Company.

6.4 **Effect of Termination.** Upon termination, Employee's obligations under Sections 3, 4, 5, and 7 of this Agreement shall survive and remain in full force and effect.

## 7. Governing Law

7.1 This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to its conflict of laws principles.

7.2 Any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement shall be resolved exclusively in the state or federal courts located in Delaware, and the parties hereby consent to the personal jurisdiction of such courts.

7.3 This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior agreements, understandings, and negotiations, whether written or oral.

**IN WITNESS WHEREOF**, the parties have executed this Agreement as of the date first written above.
