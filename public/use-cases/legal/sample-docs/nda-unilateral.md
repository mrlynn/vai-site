# Unilateral Non-Disclosure Agreement

**Effective Date:** January 15, 2025
**Agreement Number:** NDA-U-2025-0316

This Unilateral Non-Disclosure Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into by and between Acme Corp, a Delaware corporation with its principal place of business at 1200 Innovation Drive, Suite 400, San Francisco, CA 94107 ("Disclosing Party"), and the entity or individual identified on the signature page below ("Receiving Party").

## 1. Purpose

1.1 The Disclosing Party intends to disclose certain confidential and proprietary information to the Receiving Party for the sole purpose of enabling the Receiving Party to evaluate a potential business relationship, partnership, or transaction with the Disclosing Party (the "Purpose").

1.2 This Agreement sets forth the terms and conditions under which such information shall be disclosed and the obligations of the Receiving Party with respect to such information.

## 2. Definition of Confidential Information

2.1 "Confidential Information" means all non-public information disclosed by the Disclosing Party to the Receiving Party, whether before or after the Effective Date of this Agreement, in any form or medium (including oral, written, electronic, visual, or tangible), that relates to the Disclosing Party's business, technology, products, services, operations, or affairs. Confidential Information includes, without limitation: (a) proprietary software, source code, algorithms, and technical data; (b) product designs, prototypes, specifications, and development roadmaps; (c) business plans, strategies, financial data, and projections; (d) customer information, pricing models, and sales data; (e) marketing plans, competitive analyses, and partnership arrangements; and (f) any analyses, compilations, studies, or other documents prepared by the Receiving Party that contain or reflect any Confidential Information.

2.2 Confidential Information shall also include the existence and terms of this Agreement and the fact that discussions or negotiations are taking place between the Parties.

## 3. Receiving Party Obligations

3.1 **Non-Disclosure.** The Receiving Party shall hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence and shall not disclose any Confidential Information to any third party without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party.

3.2 **Restricted Use.** The Receiving Party shall use the Confidential Information solely for the Purpose and shall not use the Confidential Information for any other purpose, including for the Receiving Party's own benefit or the benefit of any third party.

3.3 **Standard of Care.** The Receiving Party shall protect the Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care that it uses to protect its own most valuable confidential information, but in no event less than a reasonable degree of care.

3.4 **Need-to-Know Basis.** The Receiving Party shall restrict access to Confidential Information to those of its employees, agents, and advisors who: (a) have a genuine need to access such information in connection with the Purpose; and (b) are bound by written confidentiality obligations at least as restrictive as those set forth in this Agreement.

3.5 **No Copying.** The Receiving Party shall not copy or reproduce any Confidential Information except as reasonably necessary for the Purpose, and all copies shall bear any confidentiality notices or legends that appear on the originals.

3.6 **No Reverse Engineering.** The Receiving Party shall not reverse engineer, disassemble, decompile, or otherwise attempt to derive the composition, structure, or underlying ideas of any Confidential Information that is provided in tangible or electronic form.

## 4. Exceptions

4.1 The obligations set forth in Section 3 shall not apply to information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate by competent evidence: (a) is or becomes generally available to the public through no act or omission of the Receiving Party; (b) was in the Receiving Party's lawful possession prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party, free of any obligation of confidentiality; (c) is lawfully disclosed to the Receiving Party by a third party who is not subject to any obligation of confidentiality with respect to such information; or (d) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Confidential Information.

4.2 **Compelled Disclosure.** If the Receiving Party is compelled by law, regulation, or legal process to disclose any Confidential Information, the Receiving Party shall: (a) provide the Disclosing Party with prompt written notice of such requirement (to the extent legally permitted) so that the Disclosing Party may seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy; (b) cooperate with the Disclosing Party in its efforts to obtain such protective order; and (c) disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that is legally required. Any Confidential Information disclosed pursuant to a legal requirement shall retain its confidential status under this Agreement.

## 5. Duration

5.1 **Agreement Term.** This Agreement shall remain in effect for a period of three (3) years from the Effective Date, unless earlier terminated by the Disclosing Party upon written notice to the Receiving Party.

5.2 **Survival of Obligations.** The Receiving Party's obligations of confidentiality and non-use with respect to Confidential Information disclosed during the term of this Agreement shall survive expiration or termination for a period of three (3) years. With respect to any Confidential Information that constitutes a trade secret under applicable law, the Receiving Party's obligations shall continue for as long as such information qualifies as a trade secret.

## 6. Remedies

6.1 **Irreparable Harm.** The Receiving Party acknowledges that any unauthorized disclosure or use of Confidential Information may cause irreparable harm to the Disclosing Party for which monetary damages alone would be insufficient.

6.2 **Equitable Relief.** In addition to all other remedies available at law or in equity, the Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief, specific performance, or other equitable remedies to enforce the provisions of this Agreement, without the necessity of posting a bond or proving actual damages.

6.3 **Cumulative Remedies.** The remedies set forth in this Agreement are cumulative and are in addition to any other remedies available to the Disclosing Party under applicable law. The exercise of any remedy shall not preclude the exercise of any other remedy.

## 7. Return of Materials

7.1 **Return or Destruction.** Upon the Disclosing Party's written request, or upon the expiration or termination of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall promptly: (a) return to the Disclosing Party all documents, materials, and other tangible items containing or embodying Confidential Information; (b) permanently delete all electronic copies of Confidential Information from the Receiving Party's systems, including backup and archival storage; and (c) destroy all notes, analyses, compilations, and other derivative materials that contain or reflect Confidential Information.

7.2 **Certification.** Within fifteen (15) days of the Disclosing Party's request, the Receiving Party shall provide a written certification, signed by an authorized officer, confirming that the Receiving Party has complied with all return and destruction obligations under this Section 7.

7.3 **Legal Holds.** Notwithstanding the foregoing, the Receiving Party may retain Confidential Information to the extent required by applicable law or regulation, or pursuant to an active legal hold, provided that such retained information shall continue to be subject to the confidentiality obligations of this Agreement.

## 8. General Provisions

8.1 **No License.** Nothing in this Agreement grants the Receiving Party any right, title, or interest in or to the Confidential Information, except the limited right to review such information for the Purpose.

8.2 **No Obligation.** This Agreement does not obligate the Disclosing Party to disclose any particular Confidential Information or to enter into any further agreement with the Receiving Party.

8.3 **Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to its conflict of laws principles.
